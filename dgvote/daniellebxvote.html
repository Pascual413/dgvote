About Danielle Herbert-Guggenheim

A Passionate Educator and Community Advocate

It is with great enthusiasm and pride in my Bronx roots that I announce my candidacy for New York City Council District 11. I’ve dedicated my career to teaching school age children and toddlers with special needs and their families.
At 37, I was honored to be handpicked to lead Harlem Middle School 864 as assistant principal, where I supervised 14 teachers and 220 students. During my three years there, I worked with my team to implement new discipline and attendance policies, improve school culture, and make a meaningful impact on the students and staff. I also had the privilege of teaching graduate-level courses at Mercy College, focusing on literacy and education for students with disabilities.
Beyond my professional roles, I’m deeply involved in my community. I serve as Vice President of The Unity Democratic Club, a board member and educational director at the James Baldwin Outdoor Learning Center, and an active member of several Bronx-based coalitions. As a former United Federation of Teachers delegate and advisor to the Bronx Zoo Teacher Advisory Board, I’ve always sought ways to blend my passion for education with community engagement. While being a single mom has been a demanding chapter of my life, it’s shaped my perspective and deepened my commitment to creating opportunities for others.
